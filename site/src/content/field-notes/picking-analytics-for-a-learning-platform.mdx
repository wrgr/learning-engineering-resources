Someone just decided the LMS needs analytics, and you're the learning engineer, so it's your problem. You have a shortlist of vendors, a budget request due Friday, and a stakeholder deck due next Tuesday. Here's what to actually care about.

## Five things that matter

### 1. Can it capture *attempt-level* data?

Not "user completed the module." That's completion. You need: "user attempted question 4 three times, chose option B twice, chose option C once, spent 47 seconds on attempt 2." If the analytics layer can't capture at the attempt level, you cannot do formative analysis. You're limited to "did they finish" and "did they pass." That's not learning engineering — that's an attendance system.

### 2. Does it emit a learner-portable standard (xAPI / Caliper)?

If the analytics layer traps data inside a proprietary format, you can't export it when the contract ends, can't combine it with data from another platform, and can't reproduce your analysis. xAPI (with a well-designed statement vocabulary) is the floor. Caliper is acceptable. Anything else means you're building a data silo you'll regret in 18 months.

### 3. Can you run your own queries?

A dashboard designed by a vendor PM will show you what *they* think matters. It will not show you what you need to debug a specific problem in a specific course. Ask: "Can I write a SQL query against the event store?" or "Can I export raw events as CSV/JSON for offline analysis?" If the answer is no, the dashboard is a brochure, not a tool.

### 4. What's the latency from event to queryable row?

If it's 24 hours, you cannot do real-time adaptive work. For most learning engineering, you don't need sub-second — but you need same-session. "Learner did X, and within 30 seconds the system responded by doing Y" requires the analytics layer to be fast enough to drive decisions, not just report them.

For post-hoc analysis (which is most of what LEs do), next-morning latency is fine.

### 5. What's the cost model?

Per-event pricing will bankrupt you at scale. Attempt-level data from 10,000 learners doing 50 questions each is 500,000 events per assessment cycle. If the vendor charges per event, multiply by every assessment, every cohort, every year. Ask for per-seat or flat-rate pricing. If they won't offer it, they haven't designed for learning data volumes.

## Three things that don't matter (yet)

- **AI-powered insights.** If you don't understand what the raw data shows, an AI summary of it will mislead you faster.
- **Pre-built dashboards for "engagement."** Engagement is not a construct. If a vendor can't define what they measure, the dashboard is decoration.
- **Integration count.** "We integrate with 200 LMS platforms" means nothing if the integration doesn't pass attempt-level data. One deep integration beats twenty shallow ones.

## What to do Monday morning

Ask each vendor on your shortlist exactly one question: "Show me a raw event record from a learner attempting a quiz question." If they can produce that in under five minutes, keep talking. If they redirect you to the dashboard, move on.
